# Group 7's Presentation
One of my favorite presentations out of all the nine groups would be group 7’s on Inclusive Terminology. This stood up to me out of all the others mainly because of how normalised certain terms are that on a deeper level have a much more biased implication. We often look at technology as a way to move forward, socially; but to see a tool for the future being used to weaponize the social hierarchy in the system is a set back from everything we have worked towards in a technological point of view.
The group explored various types of biases such as gender, racial, cultural and class based bias. I thought that it was extremely important to note the fact that these biases are not just of a certain type, but of various ones.
I agree with the group and their emphasis on the need for change and equality within the IT sector.
In terms of what the group could have done better, I would say that they could have included a view on casteist biases too. In India, caste-based bias has been a huge problem for a very long time; it still is, in undertones of certain actions. By including aspects of biases that is present uniquely in India, the project could have been a way to demonstrate the issues one can possibly face if they decide to join the IT field of occupation.

Furthermore, I think this project and the questions brought up during the presentation is a unique opportunity to demonstrate the discriminations one might face within the IT sector if one decides to follow through with the job opportunities presented by this discipline. While at first glance the job opportunities might seem like sunshine and rainbows, in a realistic sense the challenges faced by people, mainly women and people of a lower economic and social status, pose a problem to continue in this line of opportunity.
